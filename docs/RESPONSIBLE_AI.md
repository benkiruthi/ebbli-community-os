# Responsible AI Principles

AI in Ebbli Community OS should strengthen human agency and community judgment.

## Required principles

### Human responsibility

AI may assist with discovery, drafting, summarization, categorization, translation, and navigation. It must not become the unaccountable final decision-maker for high-impact actions.

### Visible uncertainty

AI output should not be presented as verified truth. Interfaces should distinguish suggestions, automated classifications, member claims, moderator decisions, and verified facts.

### Privacy and data minimization

Send only necessary information to AI providers. Sensitive documents, identity information, health details, financial evidence, private messages, and children's information require stricter review and safeguards.

### Human review

Meaningful human review is required for:

- approving or rejecting sensitive needs;
- identity and evidence verification;
- sanctions and account restrictions;
- eligibility decisions;
- safety escalations;
- decisions that materially affect access to money, services, or opportunity.

### Fairness and local context

Systems should be evaluated for language, regional, cultural, gender, disability, and socioeconomic bias. English-only performance should not be treated as sufficient.

### Explainability

Where AI affects ranking, recommendations, or moderation queues, users and moderators should receive understandable reasons where practical.

### Safety boundaries

Prompts, tools, and outputs should be scoped. The system should defend against prompt injection, unauthorized data access, unsafe actions, and attempts to bypass moderation.

## Evaluation expectations

AI features should define:

- intended use;
- excluded uses;
- known limitations;
- evaluation datasets;
- quality and safety measures;
- escalation paths;
- logging and retention rules.

## Prohibited patterns

Contributions should not:

- silently train on private community data;
- expose secrets or private records to models;
- automatically label a person dishonest or dangerous without review;
- fabricate verification results;
- conceal that content was AI-generated where disclosure matters;
- use engagement as the only recommendation objective.
